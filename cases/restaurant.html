How a restaurant chain standardized service across 10,000+ stores in three months.

An ‘AI manager’ that coaches every shift — detecting non-compliance and telling staff exactly what to do, in real time.

AIMALL TeamService Standardization4 min read

A restaurant chain is only as good as its worst shift. Across 10,000-plus locations, the same rules apply: greet the guest, wear the hat and gloves, flip the meat on time, keep the counter clean. Some stores nail it. Others quietly let it slide.

Head office couldn't be in every store, and the odd mystery-shopper visit caught problems weeks too late. Service drifted, food-safety risk crept in, and there was no realistic way to coach every person on every shift.

Standards that don't scale

You can't manually supervise 10,000 stores. A skipped greeting, a missing mask, meat left unflipped: none of it shows up until a customer complains or an inspector walks in, and by then it's too late.

+30%Satisfaction rate
−20%Labor cost
−45%Staff misbehavior

An AI manager on every shift

AIMALL set up EdgeX as a kind of always-on shift manager. It spots problems as they happen: a missing hat or mask, someone on their phone, an empty counter, meat that hasn't been flipped. Then it sends the staff on duty a short “here’s what to do” alert with a photo, usually within five seconds.

“It's like having a manager in every store, on every shift, who doesn't miss anything.”

The results

In three months across 10,000-plus stores, customer satisfaction went up 30%, labor cost came down 20%, and staff slip-ups dropped 45%. For the first time, every store delivered the same standard, shift after shift.
